# Business Impact Analysis (BIA)

After risks are identified, organizations conduct a Business Impact Analysis (BIA).  
A BIA evaluates how disruptions affect business functions and determines how quickly those functions must be restored.

A BIA helps organizations:

- Identify and prioritize critical business functions
    
- Understand the operational and financial consequences of disruption
    
- Define recovery requirements (time and data)
    
- Support disaster recovery and continuity planning
    

To perform a BIA, we use several critical metrics:

- RTO (Recovery Time Objective)
    
- RPO (Recovery Point Objective)
    
- MTTR (Mean Time to Repair)
    
- MTBF (Mean Time Between Failures)
    

Below is each term, clearly explained.

# Key BIA Metrics

---

## 1. Recovery Time Objective (RTO)

RTO defines the maximum acceptable downtime for a business process or system.  
If the system is not restored within this time, the organization will face severe consequences.

Example:  
An e-commerce website determines it cannot afford to be down for more than 2 hours before sales loss becomes critical.  
Therefore, its RTO = 2 hours.

---

## 2. Recovery Point Objective (RPO)

RPO defines the maximum acceptable data loss, measured in time.  
It answers the question: _â€œHow old can the restored data be after recovery?â€_

Example:  
A financial institution can tolerate losing only the last 15 minutes of transaction data.  
Thus, its RPO = 15 minutes, meaning backups must run at least every 15 minutes.

---

## 3. Mean Time to Repair (MTTR)

MTTR defines the average time required to repair a system or component after a failure.  
A lower MTTR = faster recovery and less downtime.

Example:  
A critical machine in a factory breaks down 5 times in a year.  
Each repair takes an average of 4 hours.  
Thus, MTTR = 4 hours.

---

## 4. Mean Time Between Failures (MTBF)

MTBF measures the average time between one failure and the next.  
A higher MTBF = better reliability.

Example:  
The same machine breaks down 5 times/year.  
This means 1 failure every 2.4 months, or approximately 72 days.  
Thus, MTBF â‰ˆ 72 days.
