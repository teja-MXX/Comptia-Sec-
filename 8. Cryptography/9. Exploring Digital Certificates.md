# ‚≠ê What Are We Actually Looking At?

When you visit **[https://google.com](https://google.com)** or **[https://apple.com](https://apple.com)**, your browser does two things:

1. **Checks whether the website is really Google/Apple**
    
2. **Builds an encrypted tunnel** so no one can spy on the data.
    

To do this, it uses a **digital certificate** (public-key certificate).

When you click the lock icon, you see the certificate details.

# üîç 2. Encryption Algorithm Used

This is the most important difference.

### **Google ‚Üí ECC (Elliptic Curve Cryptography)**

- Key size: **256 bits**
    
- Very small key, very fast
    
- Equally secure as RSA-2048
    

### **Apple ‚Üí RSA**

- Key size: **2048 bits**
    
- Older and widely supported
    
- Much heavier computationally
    

---

# ‚≠ê Why do they use different algorithms?

### **ECC (Google)**

Google prioritizes speed and performance because:

- billions of mobile users
    
- low-power phones and tablets
    
- ECC = strong security + smaller keys + lower CPU cost  
    (ECC-256 ‚âà security of RSA-3072)
    

### **RSA (Apple)**

Apple‚Äôs desktop site knows you are on a powerful device:

- more CPU available
    
- RSA is still extremely common
    
- high compatibility with all devices and software
    

**Both algorithms give strong security. They‚Äôre just different tools.**

# ‚≠ê Why does the certificate matter?

Because this is the ONLY way your browser knows:

- This website is not fake
    
- You are really talking to Google/Apple
    
- No one is intercepting your data
    
- The encryption is safe
    

Without the certificate:

- You could be connecting to a phishing page
    
- Anyone on public Wi-Fi could spy on you
    
- Encryption would not work properly
    
- Browser would show a big RED warning
